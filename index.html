About

Hello! My name is João Pedro and I am a Ph.D. student and the Karl W. Deutsch Fellow in the Department of Government at Harvard University. My research explores how international organizations survive, adapt, and evolve within complex institutional environments. I study questions of institutional design, regime complexity, the political economy and the life cycles of international cooperation. I am also broadly interested in the international dimensions of human rights and trade policy.

Before Harvard, I received my B.A. in International Relations from the University of São Paulo (USP) in 2025.

I am Brazilian, originally from Saquarema, a small coastal town near Rio de Janeiro, where I grew up surrounded by the ocean and a strong sense of community.

Research Interests

My research examines international organizations, institutional design and reform, regional and informal IOs, the crisis (and resilience) of the liberal international order.
